bath house

This design explores the intricacies of personal and open spaces within confined spaces. This bath house design focuses on such allowing for those to express themselves in a specific way that outlines their intentions. The bath house hosts a relaxing environment for those that wish to seek fulfillment, from a hard-working day to a place of inner piece this bathhouse provides such a place.

The bathhouse consists of exterior and interior pools that suit purpose within the complex, the site was designed with the intention of beautiful qualities juxtaposed with the arguably harsh climate of the everyday working world. The site is facilitated in Geelong along the Barwon River a beautiful rich environment of vegetation which provides a quality of comfort, the design facilitates such. The design seems hidden with the use of green roofs to disguise its structure, which further develops a connection with its peaceful private personality, allowing for its demographic to feel the effects of relaxation.

malt house exhibition

This design facilitates an exhibition space, using the old Malt house located in Geelong, we were free to design a facility that reflects on the site, I chose this time to develop an understanding of its history and decided to delve into an abstract thought of our five senses, being vision, hearing, touch, smell and taste. The design allows for an abstract interactive exhibition space with partition walls for development and interchangeable themes depending on its use. I Decided to not demolish the Malt house building given its rich history for Geelong, so the design interacts with the interior structure allowing for plenty of space to use creatively.

The partition walls were extremely important to my development as each room had to suite its function, for example the sound room needed to be insulated well to develop a space that purely focuses on our perception of sound. The partition walls dissected with 5 insulating properties to help provide such a feel, these include fabrics, soundproof boards, mass loaded vinyl, sound insulated wool & timber studs to allow for structure. The design focuses heavily on a deep and meaningful understanding on our senses allowing for participants to explore their mind body and soul.
